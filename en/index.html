About Aged Sake

About Jukusei Koshu

Definition of Aged Sake

Aged sake (Koshu) is sake that has been aged at the brewery for at least 3 years, excluding sake with added sugars. Through long-term aging, unique aromas and deep flavors develop, creating sake with distinct characteristics different from regular sake.

Types of Aged Sake

There are three main types of aged sake: light and refined type, rich and full-bodied type, and intermediate type. Various characteristics emerge depending on aging temperature, duration, and ingredients.

History of Aged Sake

While sake aging culture has existed for centuries, modern research and promotion of aged sake began in earnest in the 1990s. Through the fusion of traditional techniques and modern brewing science, aged sake is being established as a new sake culture.

About Our Society

About Kenkyukai

The Reserve Sake Association is a research organization where breweries, researchers, and enthusiasts gather to improve sake quality through aging and create new value. We promote the appeal of aged sake both domestically and internationally.

100-Year Sake Storage Project

Nihonshu Hyakunen Chozo Project

A project to continue storing the highest quality sake for 100 years into the future. We aim to create the ultimate aged sake that transcends time. This project serves as an initiative to preserve sake culture and build a bridge to the future.
